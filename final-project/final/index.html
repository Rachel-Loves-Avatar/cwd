Internet Consumption and Stress Levels Amongst Undergraduate Students

Social Media is a large part of an undergraduate student's life. Whether it is watching short videos on TikTok or liking photos on Instagram, almost ever undergraduate student uses the internet outside of their academic work.
However, the internet can also be a stressful place. For undergraduate students who have to deal with constant academic stress, it often serves as an escape, but it may leave them more stressed than they were before. Today, I will show you some data gathered from a survey on internet consumption and stress levels in undergraduate students.
First, the survey asked some undergraduate student how much time they usually spend on the internet a day. This ranges form 1-3 hours to more than 15 hours a day. As you can see below, most students spent less time on the internet a day. These results ended up being very surprising.
Next, I wanted to study how likely people were to use the internet as a coping mechanism. Oddly enough, there was an equal amount of students who were both more likely and less likely to use it as a coping mechanism. The chart below demonstrates this data by showing the ratings form 1-5 (1 being least likely and 5 being the most likely).
Finally, I wanted to get down to the stress levels. The survey asked undergraduate students how stressed they feel after consuming the internet and to rate their stress level on a scale from 1 - 10 (1 being the lowest and 10 being the highest). The charts show this data side-by-side to better analyze common themes.

Findings and Conclusion

Most students felt that their stress levels were unchanged or at least lowered a little. This came as a surprise since the expected results were that stress levels would be raised. One thing many students said was that when they are stressed, they choose to consume wholsome content or content that relaxes them. This explains why the results ended up the way that they were. Overall, what this tells me is that students are more aware of how internet consumption can impact their stress levels, so they are limiting their time on the internet and also choosing to view positive content to help them calm down.
